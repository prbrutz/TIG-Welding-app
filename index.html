Pulse, demystified

Know when to pulse

The app includes a full guide. The short of it:

◢ Pulsing earns its keep

  • Thin sheet. The cool background phase freezes the puddle between peaks, so you blow through less.
  • Out-of-position. The puddle solidifies instead of sagging on vertical and overhead work.
  • Distortion & cadence. Lower average heat, plus a metronome for an even, stacked-dime bead.

◣ Skip it

  • Thick plate at full heat. When you want a hot, fluid puddle, the cool phase only slows you down.
  • Fast fill passes. The cooling cycle drops the deposition rate.
  • Flat welds you control. If your puddle is dialed in, pulse adds knobs without much gain.
Current, not voltage. TIG is a constant-current process, so you pulse the amperage — arc voltage just follows your arc length. That is why the whole pulse calculator works in amps.
